# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Parents and young-to-mid adults in the Lausanne region, primarily eco-minded families with newborns or young children. Also older parents whose children have outgrown their things and want to pass them on rather than discard them. They value community, mutual trust, and sustainability over transactions. Most use the app on mobile — often one-handed while tending to a child — but some browse on desktop at home. Tech comfort varies; the interface must feel obvious on first use.

## Product Purpose

Babytroc is a community lending platform for children's items between local families. It exists to reduce waste, make parenthood more accessible, and strengthen neighborly trust. Success looks like parents naturally reaching for Babytroc before buying new — because it's easier, friendlier, and feels like borrowing from a neighbor.

## Brand Personality

Neighborly, trustworthy, helpful. The tone is warm and direct — like a friend who lives down the street offering their stroller. Not corporate, not cute, not minimal-for-the-sake-of-it. The app should feel safe and welcoming, like a shared space people actually want to return to.

## Anti-references

- Generic marketplaces (Amazon, eBay, Leboncoin): transactional, cold, impersonal. Babytroc is about sharing, not selling.
- Corporate SaaS: dashboards, metrics, upsell banners, empty productivity aesthetics. This is a community tool, not a business tool.
- AI-generated minimalism: the sterile, flattened, over-simplified look that strips away personality. Babytroc should have texture and warmth, not feel like a template.
- Children's toy store aesthetic: bright primary colors, cartoonish illustrations, playful fonts. The users are adults; the product should feel grown-up while remaining approachable.

## Design Principles

1. **Trust is earned visually.** Every screen should communicate safety and reliability through clarity, consistency, and care — not through badges or slogans.
2. **Community over commerce.** Interactions should feel like neighbors helping each other, not buyers and sellers negotiating. Language, flows, and affordances reinforce sharing, not transactions.
3. **Obvious on first glance.** Parents are busy and often distracted. Every action should be self-evident. If something needs explanation, simplify it.
4. **Warmth with substance.** The interface should feel human and inviting without being decorative or hollow. Every visual choice serves the experience.
5. **Mobile reality.** Design for one hand, partial attention, and interruptions. Desktop is a bonus, not the baseline.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Sufficient color contrast for outdoor/bright-light mobile use. Touch targets sized for one-handed operation (44px minimum). Respect reduced-motion preferences. Support screen readers through semantic HTML and ARIA where Radix Vue doesn't already provide it.
